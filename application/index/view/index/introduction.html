What is Childhood Obesity?

Obesity is the excessive or abnormal accumulation of fat that may affect health. Childhood obesity is a medical condition which effect children and teenagers.
If a child stores too much fat then they are classified as overweight or obese.

What are the complications associated with childhood obesity?

Childhood Obesity can lead to health complications such as:
- Liver diseases.
- Type 2 diabetese.
- Respiratory issues like asthma.
- Bone and joint problems.
Obesity also has some serious psychological effects like:
- Increased risk of depression.
- More likely to be bullied or teased.
- May have poor self-esteem.
- May feel socially isolated.

28% of Australian Adults were obese in 2014-2015, an increase from 19% in 1995

1 in 4 Australian children aged 2-17 years were overweight or obese in 2014-15

5% of the burden of disease was attributed to overweight and obesity in 2011.
